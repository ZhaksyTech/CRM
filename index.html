Client Management Workflow

A systematic approach to managing your client interactions from lead to successful project completion and beyond.

Phase Key Actions Notes for Zhaksytech
1 Initial Contact & Discovery Respond quickly to inquiries. Schedule an initial call to understand client's core problem, business goals, and current challenges. Listen more than you talk.
2 Needs Assessment & Solution Ideation Based on discovery call, propose 1-3 potential solutions (e.g., "A Telegram bot could automate X, or a simple web app could do Y"). Discuss feasibility and align on a primary solution.
3 Proposal & Pricing Send a detailed, personalized proposal (using your template) outlining scope, features, timeline, and investment. Be clear about what's included and any out-of-scope items.
4 Contract & Onboarding Once proposal is accepted, send service agreement. Collect initial payment. Kick-off meeting to solidify requirements, set communication channels, and introduce project tools (e.g., Trello board).
5 Development & Communication Regular updates (daily/weekly as agreed). Use project management tools for transparency. Manage expectations on scope changes. Get regular feedback on progress.
6 Testing & Revisions Present working versions for client testing. Collect feedback and implement revisions within the agreed scope. Ensure all features work as intended.
7 Deployment & Training Deploy the solution. Provide necessary training for the client/their team on how to use the bot/app/platform effectively. Hand over any credentials or documentation.
8 Post-Launch Support & Feedback Provide agreed-upon post-launch support. Schedule a feedback call 1-2 weeks after launch to ensure satisfaction. Politely ask for testimonials or referrals if they are happy.
9 Relationship Nurturing (Long-Term) Stay in touch occasionally (e.g., share relevant articles, offer future upgrades). Happy clients are your best source of repeat business and referrals.

How to Use This in Google Sheets (Recommended)

To keep this system organized and editable, we recommend setting it up in Google Sheets:

  1. 1 Create a New Google Sheets Document: Go to Google Sheets and start a blank spreadsheet.
  2. 2 Create Separate Sheets (Tabs): At the bottom of the Google Sheet, click the "+" icon to add new tabs. Name them:
    • Getting Started
    • Strategy
    • Pricing
    • Marketing
    • Outreach
    • Proposals
    • Portfolio
    • Client Management
  3. 3 Copy Content from Each Section:
    • For tables (Strategy, Pricing, Client Management), copy the table directly. Google Sheets is usually good at pasting tables correctly.
    • For text-based sections (Marketing, Outreach, Proposals, Portfolio), copy the content from each "template-box" individually and paste into relevant cells in your Google Sheet. You might use one cell for the title and the cell below for the content, or structure as a simple list.
  4. 4 Format & Customize:
    • Use **borders** for tables, **bold** for headers, and adjust **column widths** for readability.
    • Add **background colors** to rows in your Pricing sheet (e.g., light green for Basic, light blue for Pro, light red for Custom) to match the visual coding here.
    • Enable **text wrapping** for cells containing longer text descriptions to ensure everything is visible.
    • Feel free to **edit the content** to perfectly fit your voice, services, and specific client needs.
  5. 5 Save & Access: Your system is now saved in the cloud and accessible from anywhere!
